Soy Chicken

A delicious and easy-to-make chicken dish, marinated in a savory soy sauce blend and cooked to perfection.

Prep Time: 15 mins Cook Time: 25 mins Servings: 4

Ingredients

  • 500g chicken thighs, boneless and skinless, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp honey or brown sugar
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp grated fresh ginger
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch (for thickening)
  • 2 tbsp water (for cornstarch slurry)
  • Optional: Sesame seeds and chopped green onions for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, whisk together soy sauce, honey, rice vinegar, sesame oil, minced garlic, grated ginger, and black pepper.
  2. Add chicken pieces to the marinade, ensuring they are well coated. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes, or up to 4 hours for best flavor.
  3. Heat a large non-stick skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the marinated chicken (reserve the marinade). Cook chicken until browned on all sides and cooked through, about 5-7 minutes.
  4. While chicken cooks, whisk cornstarch and water in a small bowl to create a slurry.
  5. Pour the reserved marinade into the skillet with the cooked chicken. Bring to a simmer.
  6. Stir in the cornstarch slurry. Cook, stirring constantly, until the sauce thickens to your desired consistency, about 1-2 minutes.
  7. Remove from heat. Serve immediately over rice or noodles, garnished with sesame seeds and green onions if desired.
